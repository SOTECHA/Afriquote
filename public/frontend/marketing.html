Content strategy

Content that earns trust
before asking for money

African professionals are deeply skeptical of tools they haven't seen work with their own eyes. Every piece of content should answer the question: "Does this actually work for someone like me, in Lagos, billing in Naira, dealing with FIRS?"

The 5 content pillars

1 — Tax education (most shared)
"What Nigerian freelancers owe FIRS in 2026." "When does WHT apply to your consulting invoice?" "How to get your WHT credit note." People share tax content because it's useful and makes them look smart in their WhatsApp groups.
2 — Proposal and deal-winning (highest intent)
"5 reasons proposals go cold (and how to fix it)." "The WhatsApp message that doubled my proposal win rate." People who engage this content are actively trying to win clients — the highest-intent audience AfriQuote has.
3 — Product demos (fastest conversion)
60-second TikTok: "Watch me send a ₦500K invoice in 90 seconds." Screen recordings showing the exact workflow. In Pidgin for Nigeria, Twi for Ghana. People who watch demos convert at 8× the rate of those who only read about the product.
4 — Customer stories (social proof)
"Adaeze went from chasing payments to getting paid in 24 hours." Real stories from real Lagos designers, Accra consultants, and Abuja contractors. Real numbers. Real screenshots (with permission). Nothing sells an African professional like seeing another African professional succeed.
5 — Business education (trust-building)
"How to price your design services in Lagos in 2026." "The contract clause that saved a Nairobi consultant ₦800,000." "How to invoice an international client from Nigeria." Positions AfriQuote as the go-to authority on African professional business management.

Weekly content calendar

Monday — SEO blog post
1,200-word post targeting one high-intent keyword. Publish on afriquote.africa/blog. Share excerpt on LinkedIn. Repurpose into 3 Twitter posts.
Tuesday — Twitter thread
8-10 tweets on one topic. "Thread: How I track 12 proposals at once without losing any." Always ends with a product link or free tool offer.
Wednesday — TikTok / Reels video
60-second product demo or business tip. Vertical format. Hook in first 2 seconds. The timer in the Quote Builder is perfect TikTok content.
Thursday — WhatsApp group share
Drop the week's most useful piece of content (usually the blog post) into 3–5 relevant WhatsApp groups. No pitch — just value. Let the tool speak.
Friday — LinkedIn thought leadership
Founder-authored post on the business of being an African professional. Personal story + lesson + soft product mention. Builds long-term trust with the B2B audience.
Weekend — Community engagement
Reply to every comment and DM. Ask for feedback. Share behind-the-scenes of building AfriQuote. The audience should feel like they're part of building something together.

The most powerful marketing copy for AfriQuote is not an ad — it's the "Powered by AfriQuote" footer on a professional invoice that lands in a client's WhatsApp and makes them wonder: "How did they make this so fast? I need this tool."

AfriQuote growth framework — April 2026
The one metric that predicts everything
Week-1 activation rate — did the user create and send their first invoice or proposal within 7 days of signing up? Users who activate in Week 1 have 4.2× the 6-month retention of those who don't. Every onboarding decision should be optimised for this single outcome.
The early warning signal
Monthly churn above 5% is a red flag that requires immediate investigation before scaling any paid acquisition. Churn is always a product problem, never a marketing problem. Fix retention before spending on growth.
The scale trigger
When organic channels deliver a consistent LTV:CAC ratio above 5:1 for two consecutive months, scale paid ads immediately and aggressively. That is the moment the engine has been validated and money spent on acquisition compounds.
