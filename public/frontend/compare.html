Competitor profiles

What each tool actually is.

Five tools that share surface-level category labels (quoting, invoicing, proposals) but serve entirely different markets, industries, and geographies.

No overlap with AfriQuote

WeQuote

UK · AV/IT integrators · Cloud CPQ · ~2018
WeQuote is purpose-built for audio-visual and IT system integrators, offering live pricing connections to 200+ distributors and 2.5 million products from 2,000+ brands. It is the premier solution for UK professionals who install CCTV, home automation, smart home audio, and commercial AV systems — allowing them to build hardware-heavy quotes in minutes by pulling live supplier pricing directly into the platform. It integrates natively with Xero and QuickBooks.
Who buys it
UK and EU AV/IT installation companies quoting hardware-intensive projects. A Lagos freelancer invoicing a client in Naira for a branding project, a Lagos contractor logging site progress, or a Kenyan consultant tracking WHT — none of these people exist in WeQuote's universe.
UK hardware dealers AV/IT only No Africa No tax tools
No overlap with AfriQuote

QuoteWerks

USA · IT resellers & MSPs · CPQ · 30+ years old
QuoteWerks is a market-leading CPQ platform trusted by over 35,000 users in 101 countries, built for IT resellers, MSPs, and product-focused sales teams needing deep CRM and distributor integration. With 115+ connectors to HubSpot, Salesforce, ConnectWise, Autotask, Ingram Micro, TD Synnex, and QuickBooks, it automates the quote-to-invoice flow for structured product-based sales. Reviewers consistently praise the integrations but flag the user interface as dated and note it can be difficult to train new reps.
Who buys it
US and European IT resellers, VARs, and MSPs who need to pull live product pricing from Ingram Micro or Dell and push it into Salesforce. The entire platform assumes USD or EUR pricing, US/EU tax structures, and Stripe or PayPal payments — none of which helps an African professional.
US/EU IT sector 115 CRM integrations Dated UI No Africa
Partial overlap — Sites module

Jobber

Canada · North American home services · Field management · 2011
Jobber is the leading field service management platform for North American home service businesses — landscaping, HVAC, plumbing, cleaning, pest control, carpentry. It handles the full cycle from quote to job to invoice with strong iOS/Android mobile apps (4.8/4.7 star ratings), GPS fleet tracking, and automated customer communication. In 2025 they launched an AI Receptionist, offline mode, and Tap to Pay. Pricing starts at $39/month and scales to $599/month for large teams. The most common complaint is unreliable QuickBooks sync (users report ~2% of line items dropping).
Who buys it
North American residential and commercial service contractors with field crews. AfriQuote's Sites module does what Jobber does and adds 7 more modules for a fraction of the local-currency cost. Jobber has no concept of Nigerian contractors, Naira invoicing, FIRS tax obligations, or WhatsApp delivery.
North America only Home services $39–$599/mo USD No African context
Closest rival — accounting only

Xero

New Zealand · Global SME accounting · 3.95M subscribers · 2006
Xero is a global small business accounting platform with 3.95 million subscribers, strongest in Australia, New Zealand, and the UK. In August 2025, Xero launched new South African features including integrated SARS tax submissions, provisional tax tools, and enhanced mobile capabilities — and in 2026 announced a partnership with Anthropic to add AI to their platform. However, South African Xero users explicitly complain in reviews that Xero has no integration with local payment gateways — PayFast, Ozow, or PayJustNow — for transaction reconciliation. There is no Nigerian, Ghanaian, or Kenyan Xero presence, no Paystack or M-Pesa integration, no WHT calculation, and no FIRS deadline tracking.
Who buys it
The most serious global incumbent to watch. But Xero is a pure accounting platform — it does not do proposals, contracts, construction site monitoring, or business health scoring. AfriQuote covers the 7 critical things Xero doesn't while connecting to the African payment ecosystem Xero ignores.
Closest rival Pure accounting No Paystack/M-Pesa No WHT/FIRS

AutoQuotes (AQ)

USA · Foodservice equipment & supplies · CPQ · 35 years old
AutoQuotes is the dominant CPQ platform for the North American foodservice equipment and supplies (FES) industry, trusted by 20,000+ professionals including kitchen equipment dealers, manufacturers, reps, and consultants. Now part of Revalize, it offers 2D/3D kitchen design tools, AR/VR space planning, and the world's largest database of commercial kitchen equipment — 1 million+ products. Pricing starts at $700/year. The platform has recently drawn criticism for abandoning Apple device support for its mobile app, forcing Mac users to pay for Parallels just to access the software.
Who buys it
US and European restaurant equipment dealers and commercial kitchen consultants. It has been called a monopoly tool — useful only because there's no competition, not because it's great. Completely irrelevant to any AfriQuote user on any continent.
